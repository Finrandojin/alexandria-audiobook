RULES:
1. NARRATOR vs CHARACTER — The most important rule. Be strict:
   CHARACTER: ONLY words spoken aloud by a character. Dialogue, verbal replies, shouted orders — if another character in the scene would hear it, it is CHARACTER.
   NARRATOR: EVERYTHING else — descriptions, actions, thoughts, internal monologue, scene-setting, reflections. If no one else in the scene would hear it, it is NARRATOR.
   - Thoughts and internal reflections are ALWAYS NARRATOR, even when they concern a specific character.
   - NEVER convert narration into dialogue. If the author wrote it as third-person narration, it stays third-person narration read by NARRATOR.
   WRONG: Source says 'He had always been a poor liar' → {"speaker": "MARCUS", "text": "I have always been a poor liar"}
   RIGHT: Source says 'He had always been a poor liar' → {"speaker": "NARRATOR", "text": "He had always been a poor liar."}

   RIGHT: {"speaker": "NARRATOR", "text": "She turned to the window."}, {"speaker": "ELENA", "text": "I cannot do this anymore."}

     SIMPLE — Narration then dialogue:

     → {"speaker": "NARRATOR", "text": "She pulled the old map from her bag."}
     → {"speaker": "ELENA", "text": "Here — this is where they were last seen."}
     MULTI-SEGMENT — Dialogue → narration → dialogue in one paragraph:
     Source: '"Take a look at this." He slid the envelope across the table. "It arrived this morning," he said. "Read it carefully."'
     → {"speaker": "MARCUS", "text": "Take a look at this."}
     → {"speaker": "NARRATOR", "text": "He slid the envelope across the table."}
     → {"speaker": "MARCUS", "text": "It arrived this morning. Read it carefully."}
     EXTENDED — Narration → dialogue → trailing narration in same paragraph:
     Source: 'She unfolded the letter and held it to the lamp. "It is dated last Tuesday — three days before the fire." Her hands trembled as she set it down on the desk.'
     → {"speaker": "NARRATOR", "text": "She unfolded the letter and held it to the lamp."}
     → {"speaker": "ELENA", "text": "It is dated last Tuesday — three days before the fire."}
     → {"speaker": "NARRATOR", "text": "Her hands trembled as she set it down on the desk."}

3. PRESERVE THE AUTHOR'S TEXT. Do not change tense, person, or wording.
4. NARRATOR GROUPING: Keep consecutive narrator text in ONE entry unless the emotional tone genuinely shifts. Do NOT split narration into individual sentences — short narrator lines cause inconsistent TTS delivery. Only split when a real tone change occurs (e.g. reflective passage → sudden action).
